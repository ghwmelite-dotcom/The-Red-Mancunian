# EP1 Script — "I'm Rebuilding Man United With ONLY Academy Players"

**Target length:** 9–12 min
**Goal of this episode:** Sell the premise so hard that people *have* to come back for EP2. Establish the rules, the stakes, and YOU as the character. By the end the viewer should be able to say what this channel is in one sentence.

> How to use this: these are **beats, not a word-for-word read.** Talk naturally to each beat. The bracketed `[ON SCREEN: ...]` notes are text/graphics to add in editing. The `► ACTION` notes are what to be doing in-game while you talk.

---

## BEAT 1 — COLD OPEN / HOOK  *(0:00–0:30)*
Open on tension, not a greeting. **Do NOT start with "hey guys welcome back."**

Say something like:
> "Manchester United have spent over a billion pounds in the last decade… and won nothing that matters. So I'm doing the opposite. No galácticos. No panic buys. I'm rebuilding this club with academy kids and homegrown players — and I'm going to win the Champions League doing it. This… is The Mancunian Way."

`[ON SCREEN: channel logo sting + series title "THE MANCUNIAN WAY"]`
`[ON SCREEN: text flashes — "NO SIGNINGS." / "YOUTH ONLY SPINE." / "WIN THE CL."]`

**Why:** the first 15 seconds decide your retention graph. Lead with the bold claim and the stakes.

---

## BEAT 2 — THE RULES  *(0:30–2:00)*
Now a quick, energetic intro of yourself + lay out the challenge rules clearly. This is the "format contract" with the viewer.

Cover:
- Who you are / what the channel is (one line — keep it fast)
- **The Rules** (put them on screen as a list):
  1. The spine of the team is academy graduates + homegrown players
  2. No buying our way out of problems — we coach and develop
  3. One "marquee exception" allowed per season *(your call — keeps drama alive)*
  4. Win condition: a Champions League with a homegrown core
- Why United specifically — the emotional reason (Class of '92, the city, homegrown pride)

`[ON SCREEN: "THE RULES" graphic, animated list]`
`► ACTION: on the United dashboard / squad screen`

**Why:** a constraint the audience understands is what makes every future decision dramatic. Make the rules crystal clear here once, then never re-explain.

---

## BEAT 3 — MEET THE SQUAD WE'RE KEEPING  *(2:00–5:00)*
Walk through the current squad through the lens of the challenge. Who's homegrown and staying? Who's a "marquee" we tolerate? Introduce 2–3 **young players to follow** — these become the recurring cast.

Do:
- Open the squad / academy / youth players screens
- Pick out 2–3 wonderkids and hype their potential — give them a "watch this kid" moment
- Be honest about the weaknesses ("we have no striker, this is going to hurt")
- React genuinely — surprise, excitement, dread

`[ON SCREEN: player name + age + position lower-thirds as you introduce each]`
`[ON SCREEN: circle/zoom the key stat that makes a wonderkid exciting]`
`► ACTION: squad screen → youth/academy screen → individual player profiles`

**Why:** people subscribe to *characters*. Plant the seeds of the regens/youngsters viewers will get attached to across the save.

---

## BEAT 4 — FIRST DECISIONS + FIRST MATCH  *(5:00–9:00)*
Show the challenge in action. Make a couple of real decisions out loud, set a tactic, then play the first match as edited highlights with live reaction.

Do:
- Talk through ONE tactical idea and WHY (the reasoning is the content)
- Maybe a team talk for personality
- Play match 1 → cut to highlights only: key chances, goals, the drama
- React live — don't narrate flatly, *feel* it

`[ON SCREEN: scoreline graphic, minute markers on highlights]`
`[ON SCREEN: text reaction stamps — "OH MY—", "ARE YOU SERIOUS"]`
`► ACTION: tactics screen → matchday → highlights`

**Why:** proves the format works as *entertainment*, not just a menu sim. Pace is everything — cut hard, no dead air.

---

## BEAT 5 — CLIFFHANGER OUT + CTA  *(9:00–end)*
Land the result emotionally and set the hook for EP2. Then a *light* call to action.

Say something like:
> "So that's the start — [result]. We've got [problem/opportunity] and one kid who might just be special. Next episode, [tease the EP2 hook]. If you want to see this homegrown experiment actually work — subscribe, and I'll see you in the next one. Up the Reds — the Mancunian way."

`[ON SCREEN: "EP2 →" teaser frame + subscribe prompt]`

**CTA rule:** ask once, naturally, at the end. Don't beg mid-video — it kills retention.

---

## Editing checklist for EP1
- [ ] Cold open cut tight — hook lands inside 15 seconds
- [ ] Rules shown as on-screen graphic (not just spoken)
- [ ] 2–3 young players given clear lower-third intros
- [ ] Match shown as highlights only — no full 90-min sim
- [ ] On-screen text for every scoreline / age / fee
- [ ] Dead air and slow menu-clicks jump-cut out
- [ ] Audio levels checked — voice clear and consistent
- [ ] Disclaimer pasted in description
- [ ] End screen / EP2 tease added

## Common EP1 mistakes to avoid
- Starting with "hey guys" instead of the hook
- Explaining the rules 3 times (once, clearly, then move on)
- Showing the full match — viewers want the story, not the sim
- Flat narration — your reactions ARE the entertainment
- No cliffhanger — always end on a reason to watch EP2
