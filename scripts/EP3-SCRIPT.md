# EP3 Script — "This Result Could END The Save"

**Target length:** 11–15 min
**Goal of this episode:** The first real high-stakes payoff. Put the homegrown experiment on the line in one defining match, ride the drama, and deliver an emotional result (triumph or gut-punch) that turns this from "a save" into "a story." This is the episode that converts viewers into subscribers.

> How to use this: beats, not a word-for-word read. `[ON SCREEN: ...]` = editing graphics. `► ACTION` = in-game while you talk.

---

## BEAT 1 — COLD OPEN / HOOK  *(0:00–0:30)*
Open at maximum tension. State what's on the line before anything else.

> "One match. If we lose this, everything we've built with these academy kids could come crashing down — [the board pulls the plug / we're out / the season's over]. No money to fix it, no signings to save us. Just our homegrown XI, and the biggest 90 minutes of the save so far. This… is the Mancunian Way, under fire."

`[ON SCREEN: the fixture + what's at stake, big bold text — "MUST WIN"]`
`[ON SCREEN: series sting]`

**Why:** stakes are the entire episode. Make the viewer feel the weight in the first 15 seconds.

---

## BEAT 2 — WHAT'S ON THE LINE  *(0:30–3:00)*
Lay out exactly why this match matters and what failure costs.

Do:
- The context: title decider / relegation six-pointer / cup knockout / board ultimatum
- The stakes for the *mission* — what losing means for the homegrown project specifically
- Fan + board expectations (show the screens — pressure is real)
- The personal angle: your nerves, what this means to you as a fan

`[ON SCREEN: league table / cup bracket / board confidence — circle the stake]`
`[ON SCREEN: text — "IF WE LOSE: …" / "IF WE WIN: …"]`
`► ACTION: league table → board / fixtures → news inbox`

**Why:** clear, high stakes make every later moment matter. The audience must know precisely what's being risked.

---

## BEAT 3 — SELECTION UNDER PRESSURE  *(3:00–6:30)*
The hardest decisions of the save so far. Dramatize the dilemma.

Do:
- The problem(s): injury, suspension, the kid's form, a tactical gamble
- Do you trust the wonderkid from EP2 in the biggest game? Talk it through
- Pick the XI and the tactical plan — explain the reasoning and the risk
- The team talk — set the tone (calm assassin or fire them up?)

`[ON SCREEN: injury/suspension flags; the XI reveal; key tactical instruction]`
`[ON SCREEN: text — "THE GAMBLE: start the 17-year-old?"]`
`► ACTION: squad/injuries → tactics → team selection → team talk`

**Why:** the decisions create the "will it work?" tension that pays off in the match. Your reasoning is the drama.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE MATCH  *(6:30–12:30)*
The centerpiece. Full emotional rollercoaster, tightly edited.

Do:
- Play it as edited highlights — every chance, goal, turning point, near-miss
- React LIVE and honestly — let the audience feel it with you
- Mark the swing moments ("this changes everything"), the kid's involvement
- Don't flatten the drama — let big moments breathe, cut everything dead

`[ON SCREEN: live scoreline + minute; reaction stamps; name-tag goals/saves]`
`[ON SCREEN: tension beats — "85'", "WE NEED ONE MORE…"]`
`► ACTION: matchday → highlights → in-match tweaks (show a sub/shout + why)`

**Why:** this is what people came for. Pace + genuine reaction = the most shareable part of your channel.

---

## BEAT 5 — AFTERMATH + NEXT ARC  *(12:30–end)*
Land the result emotionally, then open the next chapter.

If WIN:
> "We did it — with kids. [What it secures.] This is exactly why we do it the hard way. But it doesn't stop here — [next stake/ambition]."

If LOSS:
> "That one's going to hurt for a while. [Consequence.] But this save isn't over — and how we respond is the whole story. [The fight back starts next.]"

Then:
> "If you're enjoying the rebuild, subscribe and stick with it — the best is still coming. Up the Reds, the Mancunian way."

`[ON SCREEN: result graphic + emotional payoff; "EP4 →" tease; subscribe prompt]`

**CTA rule:** one ask, at the end, earned by the drama.

---

## Editing checklist for EP3
- [ ] Stakes stated in the first 15s ("MUST WIN" energy)
- [ ] "If we win / if we lose" made explicit on screen
- [ ] Selection dilemma dramatized before the match
- [ ] Match = tight highlights, swing moments allowed to breathe, dead air cut
- [ ] On-screen scoreline/minute throughout the match
- [ ] Genuine live reaction kept in (your emotion = the product)
- [ ] Audio levels checked; disclaimer in description; EP4 tease on end screen

## Common EP3 mistakes to avoid
- Under-selling the stakes — if the viewer doesn't feel the weight, nothing lands
- A flat, evenly-paced match cut — find the 4–5 swing moments and build around them
- Hiding your reaction — restraint kills this episode; let it rip
- Resolving everything — even a win should open the next ambition
- Forgetting the through-line: it's homegrown kids doing this. Keep saying it.
