# EP2 Script — "My Wonderkid Is Already Better Than a £100m Star"

**Target length:** 10–14 min
**Goal of this episode:** Introduce the first breakout academy player — the character your audience adopts and follows for seasons. Prove the homegrown experiment can actually produce a star, and bank the first big tactical win. End on a new stake so EP3 is must-watch.

> How to use this: beats, not a word-for-word read. Talk naturally. `[ON SCREEN: ...]` = text/graphics to add in editing. `► ACTION` = what to be doing in-game while you talk.

---

## BEAT 1 — COLD OPEN / HOOK  *(0:00–0:30)*
Open on the wonderkid, not a greeting.

> "Remember the rule of this save — no signings, ever. So when your rivals are spending £100 million on a striker… you'd better hope your academy produces something special. Well. Say hello to the kid who might just change everything."

`[ON SCREEN: freeze-frame of the wonderkid + name + age + a jaw-dropping stat]`
`[ON SCREEN: series sting "THE MANCUNIAN WAY"]`

**Why:** the promise of the title ("better than a £100m star") has to land in the first 15 seconds. Show the kid, tease the proof.

---

## BEAT 2 — RECAP + THE DISCOVERY  *(0:30–3:00)*
Fast recap of where EP1 left off (the result, the problem), then pivot to scouting the academy and finding the gem.

Do:
- 10-second recap with on-screen text ("Last time: [result] — and we had no striker")
- Open the youth/academy + scouting screens; find the wonderkid
- Sell *why* they're special — the standout attributes, the potential, the comparison that justifies the title
- React genuinely — this is a "did you SEE this?" moment

`[ON SCREEN: player profile, circle/zoom the key attributes + potential rating]`
`[ON SCREEN: side-by-side — your kid's stats vs a famous expensive player ("£100M" stamp)]`
`► ACTION: squad → youth/academy → scouting report → player profile`

**Why:** this is the emotional core. The audience needs to *believe* in this kid and want to follow their story.

---

## BEAT 3 — DEVELOP & DEPLOY  *(3:00–6:00)*
Show your plan to turn raw talent into a match-winner. The reasoning is the content.

Do:
- Set training focus / individual development / mentoring (pair with a senior pro)
- Choose the role and tactic that unlocks them — explain WHY out loud
- Address the risk honestly ("he's 17, he'll be inconsistent, but the upside…")
- A team talk or selection decision: are you brave enough to start the kid?

`[ON SCREEN: training/role screens; highlight the role + key instruction]`
`[ON SCREEN: text — "THE PLAN" bullet list]`
`► ACTION: training → development → tactics → team selection`

**Why:** demonstrates you're a manager, not a clicker. Builds tension around whether the gamble pays off.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE BREAKOUT MATCH  *(6:00–11:00)*
The payoff: the wonderkid's breakout performance and your first big tactical win.

Do:
- Quick pre-match line on the stakes/opponent
- Play the match → highlights only: the kid's key moments (goal, assist, big moment) + the win
- React live to every swing — the entertainment is your emotion
- Call out the tactic working ("THAT is exactly why we set him up there")

`[ON SCREEN: scoreline + minute markers; name-tag the kid's goal/assist]`
`[ON SCREEN: reaction stamps — "GET IN!", "17 YEARS OLD!!"]`
`► ACTION: matchday → highlights → post-match player ratings`

**Why:** proves the homegrown experiment produces results, not just hope. The win earns the audience's belief.

---

## BEAT 5 — NEW STAKE + CLIFFHANGER  *(11:00–end)*
Turn the triumph into the next problem. Land the emotion, set the EP3 hook.

Say something like:
> "So the kid's a star — but here's the thing. [New stake: a huge fixture coming up / bigger clubs already watching him / an injury crisis / the board's expectations just jumped]. Next episode is the one that could make or break this entire save. If you want to see whether the Mancunian Way actually works when it matters most — subscribe, and I'll see you there. Up the Reds."

`[ON SCREEN: "EP3 →" tease frame + subscribe prompt]`

**CTA rule:** ask once, at the end, naturally.

---

## Editing checklist for EP2
- [ ] Hook lands inside 15s — wonderkid shown immediately
- [ ] EP1 recap kept to ~10s with on-screen text
- [ ] The "vs £100m star" comparison shown as a graphic (delivers the title)
- [ ] Match shown as highlights only — the kid's moments name-tagged
- [ ] On-screen text for every stat / scoreline / age
- [ ] Dead air + slow menus jump-cut out
- [ ] Audio levels checked
- [ ] Disclaimer in description; EP3 tease on the end screen

## Common EP2 mistakes to avoid
- Burying the wonderkid — they must appear in the first 15 seconds
- Over-recapping EP1 (10 seconds, then move)
- Not delivering the title's promise (show the comparison that earns "better than £100m")
- Showing the full match instead of the kid's story
- Ending flat — always hand off a bigger stake to EP3
